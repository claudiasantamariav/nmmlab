The model map

Six models, three ways: what their equations are made of, what a single node and a coupled brain network physically look like, and the ladder they build up along — starting from Kuramoto's bare phase oscillators, the leanest possible skeleton every later model adds detail to.

3The ladder, top to bottom

The same six models from the Explore page, in the order they were built.

Phase-only the starting point

θ̇ᵢ = ωᵢ + (G/N)Σⱼ sin(θⱼ−θᵢ) — phase only, no amplitude. The barest possible skeleton for synchronization.

▾
+ amplitude (a mass on a spring)

HO rung 2

ż = (α + iω)z — a linear oscillator, closed-form solution. A mass on a spring.

▾
+ cubic nonlinearity

Stuart–Landau rung 3

A cubic term caps the HO's neutral circle into a genuine limit cycle — the first real, self-sustaining rhythm.

▾
+ E/I populations, sigmoid

WILCO rung 4 · Wilson–Cowan

Two neural populations replace the abstract oscillator. Turn up the drive and the E–I loop starts oscillating on its own.

▾
+ synaptic time constants

NMM1 rung 5

Synaptic rise/decay time constants refine the frequency and shape of the rhythm that emerges from the E–I loop.

▾
+ exact QIF mean-field derivation

NMM2 rung 6 · next-gen mean field

Replaces the static sigmoid with an exact mean-field reduction of spiking QIF neurons — same E/I skeleton, but the transfer function itself now has its own dynamics.
