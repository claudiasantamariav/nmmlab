How the five models connect

The model map

Castaldo et al. show that HO, Stuart–Landau, WILCO, NMM1, and NMM2 are not five separate inventions — they're rungs on one mathematical ladder, each adding exactly one idea to the model below it. This page shows that ladder twice: once as a straight read top-to-bottom, and once as an interactive map you can click through, equation by equation.

1The ladder, top to bottom

The same five models from the Explore page, in the order they were built.

ż = (α + iω)z — a linear oscillator, closed-form solution. A mass on a spring.

WILCO rung 3 · Wilson–Cowan

Two neural populations replace the abstract oscillator. Turn up the drive and the E–I loop starts oscillating on its own.

NMM1 rung 4

Synaptic rise/decay time constants refine the frequency and shape of the rhythm that emerges from the E–I loop.

▾

NMM2 rung 5 · next-gen mean field · demo coming soon

Replaces the static sigmoid with an exact mean-field reduction of spiking QIF neurons — same E/I skeleton, but the transfer function itself now has its own dynamics.

3Building the equations

Each model is its equation. Terms are coloured by the job they do — so the same colour means the same role across every model (WILCO's sigmoid and Stuart–Landau's cubic are both the amplitude brake; HO's α and WILCO's τẋ+x are both damping). Top row is the abstract lineage, bottom row the biological one.

Each term has a bracket and a label under it. Tap a label to read, in plain words, what that piece does.
