Five mathematical models, one unifying story. This site is the interactive companion to Castaldo et al. — pick a model below to build intuition for how brain rhythms emerge, from a simple spring all the way to a full neural mass model.

The model ladder — five rungs from physics to neuroscience

HO

rung 1 · harmonic oscillator

linear · closed-form solution · a mass on a spring

The starting point: ż = (α + iω)z. One complex equation captures damping, resonance, coupled networks, and the Kuramoto phase-only limit.

Interactive+ networkKuramoto limit

Stuart–Landau

rung 2

adds nonlinearity · self-sustaining limit cycle

A cubic nonlinearity turns the HO's neutral circle into a genuine limit cycle — the orbit locks onto a fixed size no matter where it starts. The first real rhythm.

Interactive+ network

WILCO

rung 3 · Wilson–Cowan

excitatory + inhibitory populations · sigmoid · Hopf bifurcation

Two neural populations replace the single oscillator. Turn up the background drive and the E–I loop spontaneously starts oscillating — a biologically grounded rhythm.

Interactive+ network

NMM1

rung 4

explicit synaptic filtering · realistic time constants

Synaptic dynamics get their own rise and decay time constants, refining the frequency and shape of the rhythm that emerges from the E–I loop.

Interactive+ network

NMM2

rung 5 · next-generation mean field

exact QIF reduction · dynamic firing-rate/potential pair

Replaces the static sigmoid with an exact mean-field reduction of spiking QIF neurons (Montbrió–Pazó–Roxin) — same E/I push-pull skeleton, but the transfer function itself now has its own dynamics.

About this companion

Castaldo, F., de Palma Aristides, R., Clusella, P., Garcia-Ojalvo, J., & Ruffini, G. (2025). A Rosetta Stone for Neural Mass Models. Shows that five widely-used models in computational neuroscience are not separate inventions but rungs on a single mathematical ladder. This website lets you interact with each model, tune its parameters, and see exactly what changes at each step up the ladder.
